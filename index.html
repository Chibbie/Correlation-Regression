Correlation and Regression

Correlation and Regression
Intro
It is used for bivariate data , meaning involving two variables
Correlation: helps in finding association or lack of it b/w two variables x and y.
In correlation we must be careful about a cause and effect relation b/w variables as sometimes variables may seem correlated but they are influenced a third variable
Regression: predicting the value of the dependent variable corresponding to a known value of the ind. variable on the assumption of a mathematical relationship b/w the two.
Bivariate Data is data collected simultaneously. (1,2) (4,5)
Correlation
if found that change in 1 variable results in change in another then they are correlated otherwise uncorrelated or independent
Types
Positive Correlation
when variables move in same direction
Negative Correlation
when variables move in oppo direction.
Measures of Correlation
Scatter Diagram
can distinguish b/w different types of correlation but fails to measure the extent of relationship
Karl Pearson's Product Moment Correlation Coefficient
Intro
Best method for finding correlation
Provided that relationship is linear
defined as the ratio of covariance b/w the two variables to the product of SD of two variables
Properties
Unit-free measure
coeff of correlation remains unvariant under a change of origin or scale
Opposite signs only when two scales differ in sign
coeff of correlation always lie b/w -1 and 1
Spearman's Rank correlation coefficient
Intro
Correlation b/w two qualitative characteristics say beauty and intelligence
Can also be applied to find the level of agreement or disagreement b/w two judges so far as assessing qualitative characteristic is concerned
Rank correlation coefficient is easier to compute
Coefficient of Concurrent Deviations
Intro
Simple and casual method when we are not serious about he magnitude of two variables is the application of concurrent deviations
Regression Analysis
Intro
If y (profit) depends on x (investment) then it is simple linear regression. Y is dependent variable and x is independent variable/predictor.
Y depends on x will simply be called "Y on X" or y = a + bx.
A and B are constant
They are known as regression parameters
b is known as regression coefficient of y on x and is also denoted by Byx
Properties of Regression Lines
Coeff of regression change due to scale but not origin
Two lines of regression intersect at the point (xBar, yBar) where x and y are variables under consideration
i.e. the solution of simultaneous equatiosn
Coeff of correlation b/w two variables x and y is the simple geometric mean of the two regression coefficients
The sign would be common sign of the two regression coefficients
if both are negative, r will be negative and if both are positive then r would assume a positive value
Probable Error
Method of obtaining correlation coeff of population
Limit of correlation coeff is given by p = r+- P.E
r = correlation coeff from n pairs of sample observations
if r < PE there is no evidence of correlation
if r is more than 6 times of the P.E then the presence of correlation coeff is certain
Since r lies b/w 0 and 1. The probable error is never negative
Review of this chapter
The best measure of correlation is Pearson's Correlation Coeff.
limitation : only applicable when linear relationship b/w two variables.
When variables are not directly correlated but it is caused by influence of third variable then it is called spurious correlation or non-sense correlation
Correlation coeff measuring a linear relationship b/w two variables indicate the amount of variation of one variable accounted for by the other variable. A better measure for this purpose is provided by the square of correlation coeff known as coeff of determination.
r^2 = Explained variance / Total variance
value of 0.6 for r indicates that 0.6^ 2 x 100% or 36 percent of the variable is accounted for by the factor under consideration and the remaining 64% variation is due to other factors.
Co-eff of non-determination is given by 1-r^2
ratio of unexplained variable to total variance
Regression analysis can be applied unlike correlation for any type of relationship linear as well as curvilinear
Two regression lines coincide i.e. become identical when r = -1 or 1 or in other words there is either perfect negative or positive correlation b/w two variables.
if r = 0, regression lines are perpendicular to each other..
